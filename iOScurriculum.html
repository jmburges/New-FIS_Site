iOS Student Curriculum

Take your dev skills mobile.

The Basics //

The iOS course is an 10 week, full time, intensive program, designed to turn somebody with basic development skills into the equivalent skillset and experience of and entry-level iOS Objective-C developer. This course is designed to enhance a students existing development skills by introducing them to a new environment, not introduce them to development itself. Just like the Ruby class, you are required to be on campus Monday thru Friday, 9AM-6PM throughout the duration of the program. Outside of class there is plenty of work to be done, but that's the minimum.

The cost for the semester is $9,000. Grants and scholarships are also available for students from underrepresented groups like women and minorities.



The Structure //

Prework: Fundamentals

Before arriving on campus all students complete 20+ hours of prework. During this phase we go for breadth over depth. The goal is to familiarize you with some basic concepts and get everyone to a minimum baseline so that we can hit the ground running on campus.


  • Unit 1: Basic Apps

    The first unit is desgined to get you familiar with Objective-C, Xcode and the other iOS development resources. You'll leave the first day with an app in your pocket. Always Be Coding is a core tenant of the class and every day you'll work in pairs, teams and solo to hone your skills.

  • Unit 2: Real World iOS

    Learning how to throw together a few windows and views won't cut it in the real world. Learn how iOS development really works by making apps that integrate with 3rd party APIs and SDKs. Hear from guest speakers on how the best apps were made and implement them yourself.

  • Unit 3: Project

    You put the hard work into learning iOS development, now's the time to show your chops. Work with teammates to make an app you'll be proud of. At the end of the unit, submit your app to the App Store and show it off to your friends. All projects have to meet a minimum number of technologies/techniques used, and all projects will be open sourced.




Become a Professional iOS Developer

Objective-C

The language that started it all. Learn your way around Objective-C from ABAddressBookBuffer to Zoomscale.

Debugging

New language means new debugging issues. Learn to quickly squash those bugs using Instruments and GDB.

Universal Apps

Even your Dad has an iPad now. Learn how to combine layouts into one app, or split your app up for that extra special iPad experience.

Xcode

A craftsman knows their tools. As a budding craftsman you'll understand all the nooks and crannies of Xcode, and bend it to your will

App Distribution

Submitting to the App Store can be difficult. By the end of the class, you'll have an app submitted to the app store. Get ready to show it off to all your friends.

Mobile Design

A different form factor calls for different design fundamentals. Create apps that people want to use and explore.

Data Stores

Whether it's CoreData, the Foursquare API or your own custom API. Working with data stores is cruciul to the best apps.

SDKs

Build on the shoulders of giants. Integrate other's code into yours and say thanks with a pull request.

Custom Interfaces

iOS apps that just use the default Interfaces are boring. Turn your designer friends' wildest dreams into something to touch and interact with.

Learn Best Practices

Git Control

Your code will be safely tucked away into Git and you'll collaborate with the world through Github. Tame the rainbows and work fluidly with teams, both remote and onsite.

Test Drive It

Whether test-driven or behavior-driven, testing is a crucial part to professional software development. You'll assert your way to confident code with continuously integrated test-suites.

Agile Methodology

Agile is more than just a development practice, it's a philosophy for life. You will iterate your way to being a Scrum Master.

Join the developer community

Open Source

You will become part of the phenomenon of open source culture by contributing code and documentation to a variety of projects.

Blogs and Decks

The best way to learn is to teach. Throughout the semester you will be writing blog posts on what you're learning and making presentations at meetups.

Coder Credentials

Finally, you'll have all the street cred you'll need, complete with a StackOverflow reputation, HackerNews account, and CoderWall endorsements.
