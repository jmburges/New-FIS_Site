Become a Flatiron Mentor

"The manatee has become the mento." Tracy Jordan

In an effort to further our students engagement with the community and prepare them for a career in code, we're starting a mentoring program. Being a mentor is part of being a programmer. It helps you articulate and solidify your knowledge of code, clarify thinking about your career, expand your professional network, and provides valuable leadership skills. It also does all that for your mentee too, so it's an extremely valuable program.

Responsibilities

  1. Having a lunch (on us of course) with an eager beginner developer.
  2. Come watch them present at NYC on Rails once a semester.
  3. Take them to a technical meetup like NYC.Rb or RubyMotion NYC
  4. One pairing session (2-4 hours) on one of their projects.
  5. Answer the occasional email they might send :-)
  6. Bring them to work for a day and let them shadow you, heck, maybe even pair.
  7. Provide meaningful career guidance, such as recommending great books, screencasts, or blog posts to reads, suggesting favorite conferences, meet ups, events, and hackathons to attend, and inspiring them with stories of your projects, code, and career. Be a guide.
  8. Write a blog post about the experience.

All told, we think a mentor would spend 15-20 hours with the student over the course of the semester. Additionally, we ask that mentors agree, internalize, and practice our Code of Conduct.

The Flatiron School mentorship is going to be an amazing experience and we see it as a way for you to give back to the community by ushering in a new generation of passionate and diverse programmers. Mentor the change in the community you want to see. Plus, we're going to throw all the mentors a big party at the end.

Some more details:

  • Mentorship for the Ruby 003 Semester will be Oct 7th - Jan 13th.
  • Applications close Oct 1st. You'll be notified by Oct 4th, on a rolling basis from the day you apply.
  • Mentors will be paired with students by the Flatiron School.
  • Your name and your companies logo with URL will be added to a Mentors page on FlatironSchool.com

The Benefits:

  • Knowing something and teaching something are one in the same. Master your craft through mentorship.
  • Gain some serious leadership skills and creds.
  • Expand your professional network by working with our amazing community of students, alumni, teachers, mentors, and employers.
  • Raise you (and your company's) profile by being listed as a mentor on our website.
  • Give back to your less experienced self by teaching someone what you wish you would've known. Change a life.
  • A free lunch and party.
